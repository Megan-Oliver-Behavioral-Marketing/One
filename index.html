Most marketing strategies are built on assumptions about who people are. Mine are built on understanding how context shapes what people actually do — and why that difference changes everything downstream.

"Context isn't a detail. It's the variable that makes the same person behave completely differently."

Over the past decade I've developed a framework for brand strategy that treats audience behavior as dynamic — not fixed. The same person who ignores your ad on Monday might be your most receptive prospect on Thursday. The question isn't who they are. It's what state they're in when they encounter your brand.

That behavioral lens is what I bring to every engagement — from brand positioning and campaign architecture to go-to-market strategy and content systems.
